Orpheus Singers: Works by Tomás Luis de Victoria

Co-engineered with Kevin Allswede

Kevin Allswede and I were given the opportunity to record the University of Michigan Orpheus Singers performing works by Tomás Luis de Victoria.

The singers chose to sing in two concentric circles around the microphones. We used a blumelein configuration for the main stereo pair and added two room mics to add space.

The recordings are posted in the University of Michigan SMTD media gallery as well as on my soundcloud, shown below. The project is described in more detail here.

Swingset

Composed and arranged in collaboration with Kevin Allswede

Kevin and I composed this piece over the summer and later recorded it in the Audio Studio. In this track, we recorded each part individually to a click and added them together in post. Kevin is featured on piano and I play flute.

Cache

Audio Engineer and Sound Designer for Student Film.

Directed by David Vallejo; Produced by DeSean Grice; Written by Billy Finkenstaedt

I was the Audio Engineer for this student film in the spring of 2013. I was in charge of recording dialogue on set, mixing effects, dialogue, and music during post, and composing the minimal score. One of the more fun parts of the process was recording the sound for the car crash. We went to the local dump to record ourselves knocking over large metal boxes.

Psalm 100

Composition for SATB choir, piano, and flute

I was commissioned by Carol Tralau to compose a piece for Sanctuary Choir, the adult choir at St. Paul's United Methodist Church. My goal when writing this piece was for it to be fun to sing and listen to, but very accessible for all ability levels.
